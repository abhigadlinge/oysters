training flow and content

The program will start with VILT sessions for total of 8 hours. The sessions are of 2 hours each. The training program will engage participants with various activities such as Q&A, Individual and group activities, presentations, discussions, etc. And in the final session the participants will also be asked to present their learning through a Presentation.

M1 - Effective C-Suite Speaking:

  • Know Your Audience
  • SAPAE Model
    • S - survey
    • A - Analyze
    • P - Plan
    • A - Act
    • E – Evaluate

Session Breakdown:

• Identifying the needs and understanding and characteristics of the audience.

• Effectively conveying the message in a clear and concise manner, using techniques such as simplifying complex ideas and using visual aids.

• SAPAE Model: A framework for effective communication.

• Individual activities will be taken during the sessions along with micro learnings & Post session assignment.

• An assignment based on the SAPAE model to present in Session 2

M2 - Communicating with Impact in the Boardroom:

  • Strategies
  • Avoiding Clichés
  • Avoiding Jargon and Other Language Pitfalls
  • Navigating Difficult Conversations: Avoiding Macroaggression’s in the Boardroom
  • Denotative & Connotative words
  • Use non-sexist language

session breakdown

• Introduction to "Communicating with Impact in the Boardroom" and provide an overview of the key subtopics that will be covered during the session.

• Discussion on strategies for communicating effectively: Use of clear and concise language, Active listening, and Effective body language.

• Discussion on the importance of avoiding clichéd language, Jargons & pitfalls in the boardroom and provide examples of common phrases to avoid. Along with live examples & activities

• Navigating difficult conversations in the boardroom, how to avoid macroaggression’s: such as racism, sexism, and discrimination.

• Importance of using non-sexist language in the boardroom, including avoiding gendered terms and using inclusive language.

• Activity: Engage in a group activity that allows participants to practice the skills and strategies discussed during the session.

M3 - Power of Grooming & Do’s & Don'ts of Presentation in a Boardroom

  • POPTA Presentation Model
    • Purpose
    • Organization
    • Preparation
    • Time
    • Audience
  • How to handle Q&A.
  • Non-Verbal Effective Communication.

Session Breakdown:

• Introduction to Power of Grooming, Do’s & Don'ts of Presentation, and importance of creating a good impression in a professional setting

• Concept of the POPTA model, Discussion on each component of the model

• Applications of POPTA model in a boardroom setting, and answering questions effectively and professionally.

• Non-Verbal Effective Communication importance, Impact and examples.

M4 - Presentation & Feedback Session:

  • This will involve creating a presentation & a report
  • Driven by the participants and lead by the trainer
  • Feedback, Key takeaways, Learning & Conclusion

Course Objective

  • Developing confidence and polish as a public speaker: This might involve working on stage presence, voice and language use, and the ability to connect with an audience.
  • Enhancing communication skills: This could include learning how to effectively convey complex ideas, deliver persuasive arguments, and handle difficult questions.
  • Building leadership presence: A C-suite speaker training program might also focus on helping participants project confidence, credibility, and authority when speaking in public.
  • Enhancing presentation skills: Participants might also work on creating and delivering compelling presentations, including the use of visual aids and other multimedia tools.
  • Overall, the goal of a C-suite speaker training program is to help participants become more effective, confident, and influential communicators in a variety of settings.

    2. Participants are expected to attend the training attentively and do participate in them.

    3. Recommended batch size for this program is 25-30 participants.
